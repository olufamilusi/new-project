Job description
Linux Systems Engineer

Job Category: Information Technology

Time Type: Full time

Minimum Clearance Required to Start: None

Employee Type: Regular

Percentage of Travel Required: Up to 10%

Type of Travel: Local

Job Description:

Do you #! /bin/bash first and ask questions later? Does the idea of unmeshed switched fabric keep you up at night? Do you lurk r/homelab? Have you convinced your friends and neighbors they need automated offsite backups? Then you might be interested in a role where you can use your Linux, hardware, and networking experience to help build cutting-edge integrated digital signal processing systems. Your skills will be put to work in an Agile environment to help support our customers' Electronic Warfare mission.

What you'll get to do:
• Work closely with Digital Signal Processing engineers to build, deploy, and troubleshoot signal processing applications
• Work with hardware engineers to integrate and test new hardware and firmware systems and configurations
• Take ownership of system configuration management, maintain software baselines, and prepare software and hardware releases
• Leverage your Linux experience to propose and implement operating system configurations and optimizations
• Troubleshoot and optimize system communications leveraging TCP and UDP for unicast and multicast messaging
• Contribute to an extensive baseline of frontend and backend software suites written in python and C++
Required qualifications:
• Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Information Technology or related field
• Linux (Red Hat) administration and configuration expertise
• Knowledge of networking, routing, and firewalls
• Familiar with hardware interfaces and protocols
• Experience writing and maintaining scripts written in at least one of bash, python
Desired qualifications:
• DoD 8570.1 certification (Security+, CISSP, etc)
• Knowledge of building, managing, and orchestrating containerized applications (docker, podman, etc)
• Experience working in Continuous Integration environments
• Experience with configuration management tools (Ansible, puppet, etc)
• Familiarity with security requirements for classified environments
• Exposure writing and testing code for large software application stacks
